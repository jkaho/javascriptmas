Avoid Obstacles

Find the smallest number that can 'jump' through your array of straight line coordinates without hitting a number (the numbers at each coordinate represent obstacles to the jump).

Example: The result of the array [5, 3, 6, 7, 9] would be 4.

Explanation:
A jump of 1 creates the jump sequence: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, etc., meaning it will hit all numbers in the array.
A jump of 2 creates the sequence: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10, etc, meaning it hits 6.
A jump of 3 creates the sequence: 3, 6, 9, 12, 15, etc, meaning it hits 3, 6, and 9.
A jump of 4 creates the sequence: 4, 8, 12, 16, 18, etc, meaning it does not hit any of the numbers in the array.
